CA Repeat- Week 12 (worth 15% of final module mark) :

  • The OS1 2025 CA Repeat is a closed-book individual-based in-lab Brightspace Quiz Assessment. It will consist of a mixture of multiple choice questions, fill-in-the-blank questions, short questions, calculation questions and matching questions.  There will be no negative marking.

  • Where and When:  Wednesday 30th April 2025 @ 12pm in Lab CQ-233.  There is only one sitting of the CA Repeat and this is it!  There will be no resit opportunities.

  • Who can sit the CA Repeat?
    Students can sit the CA Repeat if and only if:
    • They did not sit CA1 or CA2.  (if you missed both CA1 and CA2, the Repeat CA only covers CA1)
    • AND They have a valid serious and significant reason for missing CA1 or CA2
    • AND They have emailed the lecturer requesting to sit the CA Repeat.  The email must state:
      • Full Student details (name, id, class group, lab group assignment).
      • Which CA they missed: CA1 or CA2.
      • The serious and significant reason why the missed the CA1 or CA2.
  • For the duration of the CA Repeat, students are only permitted to access Brightspace (to sit the CA Repeat only, not to reference lecture/lab materials) and permitted to access the Microsoft Excel, the Google Cloud Shell and the Windows Command Line Console.  Access to all other resources (physical or digital) are prohibited (students are not allowed to access previously created documents or files either in physical form or any where else).

  • The CA Repeat will begin at exactly 12:05pm  It will have a total duration of 85 minutes.  It will start on time and finish on time, regardless of whether students arrive late (or not at all).  If a student arrives late or has problems logging on, that is on them - there is no opportunity to extend the quiz for the student.  Students may leave the room when they have completed and submitted the CA Repeat.  It is the student's responsibility to ensure they have correctly submitted their CA Repeat. 

  • Toilet breaks are not permitted during the CA Repeat.  Students must ensure they have available of the toilet facilities beforehand and then logon to the desktop PC in good time to sit the CA Repeat.

  • All students must put their phones on silent and then place them away in their bags after successfully sign-in to Brightspace. No phones/pens/paper/etc are permitted on the desks.  No USB keys/drives may be plugged into the desktops/laptops.

  • The CA Repeat will consist of three components:

    • A Lecture assessment component worth 6% of final module mark consisting of 18 questions in total:
            8 questions examining lectures and additional reading materials covered by CA1 (each question worth 0.375 marks).
            10 questions examining lectures and additional reading materials covered by CA2 (each question worth 0.3 marks).

    • A Lab assessment component worth 7.8% of final module mark consisting of 23 questions in total:
            12 questions examining lab activities covered by CA1 (each question worth 0.375 marks).
            11 questions examining lab activities covered by CA2 (each question worth 0.3 marks)

    • A practical question examining CPU Scheduling algorithms consisting of one question worth 1.2 marks:
            It will consist of 4 sub questions (each sub question worth 0.3 marks).

  • What is examinable in the CA Repeat?

    • The whole module. 

    • 50% of the CA Repeat will consist of what was examinable by CA1

    • The other 50% of the CA Repeat will consist of what was examinable by CA2.

  • The CA Repeat must be completed on the Desktop PCs in the lab.  Only those students who cannot access a desktop because none are physically available must seek the explicit permission of the Lecturer to sit the CA Repeat using their own laptop, before they are allowed to do so.

  • For the duration of the CA Repeat , the Internet browser, the Google Cloud Shell, the Windows Console and Microsoft Excel are the only applications on the desktop/laptop permitted to be running or active. Within the browser application, only two tabs may be open: one tab for Brightspace and one tab for the Google Cloud Shell.  Students are only permitted to access Brightspace to sit the CA Repeat, not to access lecture/lab materials or any other resources.

  • No pens/papers/notes may be on the desk during the CA Repeat . No cheating/copying/plagiarism or AI assistance of any kind is permitted.

  • If you have any questions concerning the CA Repeat, please ask the lecturer during the scheduled lectures so other students may benefit from hearing the answer.
